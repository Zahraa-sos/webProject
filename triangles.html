What is a triangle?

A triangle is a three-sided polygon that consists of three sides and three vertices.

What is an angle?

There are three angles in a triangle. These angles are formed by two sides of the triangle, which meets at a common point, known as the vertex. The sum of all three interior angles is equal to 180 degrees. So, you should always remember that the sum of angles in a triangle is 180 degrees.

Types of angles:

  1. Acute Angle: An angle that measures less than 90 degrees.
  2. Right Angle: An angle that measures exactly 90 degrees. It forms a perfect "L" shape.
  3. Obtuse Angle: An angle that measures more than 90 degrees but less than 180 degrees.
  4. Straight Angle: An angle that measures exactly 180 degrees. It forms a straight line
  5. Reflex Angle: An angle that measures more than 180 degrees but less than 360 degrees.
  6. Full Reflex: An angle that measures exactly 360 degrees. It represents a complete turn.

Perimeter:

The perimeter is the distance around the edge of the triangle: just add up the three sides:

So, the perimeter of triangle ABC = 5+4+3=12cm

Area:

The area is half of the base times height.

  • "b" is the distance along the base
  • "h" is the height (measured at right angles to the base)

Area = ½ × b × h

The formula works for all triangles.

Note: a simpler way of writing the formula is b.h/2

Area = ½ × b × h = ½ × 6 × 5= 15 cm2

Types of triangles:

1.Right Triangle: A right-angled triangle (also called a right triangle) has a right angle (90°) in it.

-in this triangle, the angle A is the right angle, which means its measure is 90 degrees. -the side facing the right angle is called the hypotenuse, and it is the longest side.


Pythagoras' Theorem:

Over 2000 years ago there was an amazing discovery about triangles: Pythagoras said that In a right angled triangle:
the square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides.

Let's see if it really works using an example:

32 + 42 = 52
9 + 16 = 25
25 = 25
It worked!

2. Isosceles Triangle: It is a triangle that have two equal sides and two equal angles

In this triangle: AB = AC, and the angles B and C are equal.

3. Equilateral Triangle: A triangle with all three sides of equal length. All the angles are 60

The measure of each angle is 60 degrees is because of the sum of angles in a triangle:
As we just said that the sum of angles in a triangle is 180 degrees, and the angles in an equilateral triangle are equal, so 180/3 = 60.
